Spring Season

Spring is one of the four seasons, typically lasting from March to May in the Northern Hemisphere and from September to November in the Southern Hemisphere. It is the season of new beginnings, growth, and renewal, as the weather starts to get warmer and plants begin to bloom.
During spring, the days start to get longer, and the weather gradually warms up, making it a popular season for outdoor activities such as gardening, hiking, and sports. It is also a popular time for various cultural celebrations, such as Easter and Passover.
In many cultures, spring is associated with rebirth, renewal, and fertility, and is celebrated with various traditions and rituals. Some of the traditional symbols of spring include flowers such as daffodils and tulips, baby animals such as lambs and chicks, and brightly colored eggs.
Overall, spring is a season of optimism and new beginnings, marking the end of winter and the start of warmer and brighter times ahead.
